About

HIGHWAY CAFE

Did you know? The Dutch were the first to transport and cultivate coffee commercially…all because they smuggled a coffee plant out of the unyielding Arab port in 1690! With this single act of rebellion, they grew coffee in large amounts and helped cement a trading center in Amsterdam.

They soon became known among the European royals for their coffee gifts.

One such recipient was Louis XIV who planted and cultivated his “Noble Tree” in the royal gardens in 1714. High-spirited and full of flavor, the jovial nature of coffee inspired others to spread the joy (even at the risk of rebelling!) While on leave in Paris, a naval officer stole a sprout and transported it back to Martinique.

This one particular tree is believed to be the father of many Coffea arabica trees alive today in the former French colonies of Central and Latin America.

In 1727, an opportunity arose when Brazilian Lieutenant Colonel Francisco de Melo Palheta was sent to resolve a conflict between the French and Dutch colonies in Guiana. Amidst his affair with the wife of French Guianas governor, she presented M. Palheta with a farewell bouquet where she concealed fertile seeds of coffee. These seeds would turn Brazil into one of the worlds largest coffee-producing countries! Coffee now became a drink of the people.

This was the first step that helped bring coffee to us, to you, and the world. As you sip on your beloved brew in your signature Starbucks cup, you appreciate the value of a good cup of coffee — but you dont always get the opportunity to reflect on everything that went into bringing that to you.

Thats what were hoping to change with this mini-series.
